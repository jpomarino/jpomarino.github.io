About Me

I'm a computational biologist trained at Columbia University, where I earned both my B.Sc. and M.Sc. in Biomedical Engineering with a concentration in Computational Biology and Machine Learning (GPA 4.08).

My research spans single-cell RNA sequencing, cross-species data integration, generative modeling, and CRISPR-based functional genomics. I'm particularly interested in how transcriptomic state space can be navigated, predicted, and perturbed — and what that tells us about disease and regeneration.

I'm fluent in Python and R, comfortable with cloud-scale genomics pipelines, and equally at home at a wet-lab bench. I'm currently based in Virginia and actively seeking PhD programs in computational biology.

Research History

Graduate Research Assistant

Woappi + Dumitrascu Groups · Columbia University

Developed a probabilistic framework using canonical correlation analysis to predict perturbed human wound healing transcriptomics from mouse temporal scRNA-seq data. Co-authored a preprint on the Patches representation learning framework (Beker et al., bioRxiv 2024).

Jun 2022 – May 2023

Summer@SEAS Research Fellow

Chemical Genomics Laboratory · Columbia University

Built end-to-end Bash/R pipelines for sci-Plex combinatorial chemical screens. Analyzed scRNA-seq data from EGFR signaling and kinome-wide perturbation experiments in glioblastoma lines. Contributed to a preprint on pharmaco-transcriptomic heterogeneity.

Jun 2021 – May 2022

SURF Research Fellow

Genome Integrity Laboratory · Columbia University

Validated hits from a combinatorial CRISPR screen targeting 170 DNA damage response genes to identify synthetic lethal interactions for cancer therapy, using color-competition cell viability assays and standard molecular biology techniques.

Sep 2020 – May 2021

Undergraduate Research Assistant

Computational Cancer Biology Lab · Columbia University

Processed scRNA-seq and scATAC-seq data on Google Cloud Platform using the MetaCell framework to identify broad cell states in acute myeloid leukemia progression.

May 2020 – Sep 2020

Undergraduate Research Assistant

Laboratory for Embryomimetics · Columbia University
